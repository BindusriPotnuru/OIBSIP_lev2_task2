Mahatma Gandhi

1869 - 1948

"Be the change that you wish to see in the world."

Biography

Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi was an Indian lawyer, anti-colonial nationalist, and political ethicist who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule, and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā, first applied to him in 1914 in South Africa, is now used throughout the world. Gandhi's philosophy of Satyagraha, emphasizing truth and nonviolence, profoundly influenced global leaders and civil rights movements. His relentless dedication to social justice, equality, and the upliftment of the marginalized demonstrated his unwavering commitment to human dignity. Gandhi's legacy continues to inspire countless individuals to pursue peace, justice, and nonviolent change.
